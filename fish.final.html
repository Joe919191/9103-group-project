9103 Group Emergence Design: Fish Flocking Simulation

Design Brief

This project focuses on simulating fish swimming in water. We employ several rules
to simulate the movement behaviors of the fish based on their relative distances.
As a result of the phenomenon known as the "herding effect," when fish form cohesive
groups, their colors become the same.

Rules applied

1.Separation: steer to avoid crowding local flockmates;
2.Alignment: steer towards the average heading of local flockmates;
3.Cohesion: steer to move towards the average position (center of mass) of local flockmates;
More complex rules can be added, such as obstacle avoidance and Conformity Effect.
